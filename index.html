ESPR + Digital Product Passport
Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation · EU 2024/1781
Effective Jul 2026
Replaces the 2009 Ecodesign Directive and extends sustainability requirements to nearly all physical products sold in the EU. Mandates Digital Product Passports (DPPs) that carry lifecycle, material, repairability, and recyclability data. Bans destruction of unsold textiles and footwear from July 2026. Product-specific delegated acts roll out 2026–2030 covering batteries, textiles, electronics, furniture, steel, and aluminium.
Regulatory body
European Commission
Effective date
19 Jul 2026 (first obligations) · DPP registry live
Est. companies in scope
All EU market manufacturers, importers & dealers — tens of thousands
Markets in scope
All 27 EU member states · global exporters to EU
Target industries
Automotive / EV Electronics Industrial mfr Textiles Metals / steel Furniture
Non-compliance risk
Products without a DPP cannot be placed on the EU market. Market surveillance authorities can withdraw or ban non-compliant goods. Fines set nationally — expect proportionate + dissuasive penalties. Reputational risk from public enforcement actions.

End-of-life disposition data Digital product passport platforms Serialization & labeling software Lifecycle data management Supply chain traceability tools
EU Battery Regulation + Battery Passport
Regulation EU 2023/1542 on batteries and waste batteries
Passport mandatory Feb 2027
Establishes lifecycle requirements for all batteries placed on the EU market — covering carbon footprint, recycled content, supply chain due diligence, and end-of-life collection and recycling targets. From Feb 2027, EV batteries, industrial batteries above 2 kWh, and LMT batteries must carry a digital battery passport accessible via QR code. Carbon footprint declarations required from Feb 2025 for EV batteries.
Regulatory body
European Commission + member states
Effective date
Carbon footprint declaration: Feb 2025 · Battery passport: Feb 2027
Est. companies in scope
All battery manufacturers, EV OEMs, importers with turnover >€40M
Markets in scope
All 27 EU member states · global EV & battery exporters to EU
Target industries
Automotive / EV Battery manufacturers Mining & metals Industrial machinery Electronics
Non-compliance risk
Batteries without a passport cannot legally be placed on the EU market or put into service from Feb 2027. Products stopped at the border, forced market withdrawal, and national fines. Due diligence violations carry additional legal liability.

Battery end-of-life recovery Battery passport platforms Supply chain due diligence tools Carbon footprint calculation software Raw material traceability platforms
CSRD
Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive · EU 2022/2464
Active — wave 1 reporting now
Requires large EU companies to report detailed sustainability information under the European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS), covering climate, biodiversity, social, and governance topics. Replaces the NFRD. Wave 1 (largest companies, 500+ employees) are already reporting for FY2024. Scope narrowed by EU Omnibus in Feb 2026 — now applies to companies with 1,000+ employees and €450M+ revenue for FY2027 onwards.
Regulatory body
European Commission · EFRAG (standards)
Effective date
Wave 1: FY2024 reports (2025) · Wave 2 delayed to FY2027
Est. companies in scope
~50,000 EU companies (revised down from ~50K after Omnibus)
Markets in scope
EU + non-EU companies with significant EU revenue (>€450M)
Target industries
All large manufacturers Utilities Mining & metals Financial services Oil & gas
Non-compliance risk
Member state penalties vary — typically fines + mandatory corrective disclosure. Erodes investor confidence and disrupts access to capital markets. Large companies face audit requirements; false reporting risks criminal liability in some jurisdictions.
Solutions
Circular disposal & recovery data Carbon accounting software ESG reporting platforms Sustainability data management Scope 3 emissions tracking
CBAM
Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism · EU Regulation 2023/956
Definitive phase Jan 2026
Puts a carbon price on imports of carbon-intensive goods into the EU, equivalent to what EU producers pay under the Emissions Trading System (ETS). Prevents carbon leakage — where EU manufacturers move production abroad to avoid carbon costs. Transitional reporting phase ran Oct 2023–Dec 2025. Financial obligations (certificate purchases) begin Jan 2026. Scope expanding to more sectors by 2030.
Regulatory body
European Commission · EU Customs
Effective date
Transitional: Oct 2023 · Financial obligations: Jan 2026
Est. companies in scope
All EU importers of covered goods above 50-tonne threshold
Markets in scope
EU importers · global exporters of steel, cement, aluminium, fertilizer, hydrogen, electricity
Target industries
Steel & metals Aluminium Chemicals / fertilizer Industrial machinery Cement Hydrogen
Non-compliance risk
Failure to surrender CBAM certificates: penalty of €100 per tonne of unreported CO2 equivalent, plus mandatory corrective filing. Repeat violations risk import suspension. Inaccurate reporting during transitional phase also carries fines.
Solutions
Carbon-efficient asset disposal Embedded emissions tracking software Customs compliance platforms Internal carbon pricing tools
EU Right to Repair Directive
Directive EU 2024/1799 · Common rules promoting repair of goods
Effective Jul 2026
Requires manufacturers of in-scope products to offer affordable, timely repair services for up to 10 years after last sale. Consumers gain the right to choose independent repair over replacement. Manufacturers must provide spare parts and repair information to independent repairers at reasonable prices. Scope currently covers household appliances, electronics, and displays — expanding to furniture and textiles. Drives longer product lifecycles and creates secondary market opportunities.
Regulatory body
European Commission · national consumer authorities
Effective date
31 Jul 2026 (member state transposition deadline)
Est. companies in scope
All EU manufacturers & importers of in-scope product categories
Markets in scope
All 27 EU member states · appliances, electronics, displays, phones, tablets
Target industries
Electronics mfr Industrial equipment Consumer appliances Telecom devices Displays
Non-compliance risk
Member state fines up to €3,750 per violation + potential criminal liability in some jurisdictions. Manufacturers refusing to supply spare parts face consumer protection enforcement. Reputational damage in EU markets where circular economy is a purchasing factor.
Solutions
Spare parts sourcing & recovery Parts traceability & serialization Repair marketplace platforms Asset lifecycle management software
